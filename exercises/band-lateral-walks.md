---
name: Band Lateral Walks
equipment: band
muscles: glutes, hips, legs
difficulty: beginner
sets: 2-3
reps: 10 steps each way
---

Activates hip abductors and glute medius. Essential for hip stability and knee health.

## Instructions
1. Place band around ankles or just above knees
2. Stand with feet hip-width apart, slight squat
3. Keep tension on band
4. Step sideways with right foot
5. Follow with left foot (maintain tension)
6. Take 10 steps right, then 10 steps left
7. Keep knees pushing out against band

## Key Points
- Stay in slight squat position throughout
- Don't let knees cave inward
- Maintain band tension - don't let feet get too close
- Control the movement, don't bounce
- Chest stays up, back stays straight

## Benefits
- Strengthens hip stabilizers
- Improves balance
- Helps prevent knee pain
- Great warm-up before lower body work
- Activates often-neglected glute muscles

## Band Placement
- **Above knees**: Easier, good for beginners
- **Around ankles**: Harder, more hip work

## Perfect For
- Warm-up before squats/deadlifts
- Injury prevention
- Building hip strength
- Anyone with knee concerns
